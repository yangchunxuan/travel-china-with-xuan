# UK passport holders: China visa-free policy source log

Last checked: 24 July 2026

This log supports the English guide at
`/guides/china-visa-free-uk-citizens-2026/`. It is an editorial maintenance
record, not legal advice.

## Current decision

- Holders of valid ordinary UK passports may enter mainland China without a
  visa from 00:00 on 17 February 2026 through 24:00 on 31 December 2026
  (Beijing time).
- The maximum stay is 30 days for business, tourism, family or friend visits,
  exchange and transit.
- The 30-day count begins on the day after entry and runs for 30 calendar days.
- Work, formal study, journalism and similar purposes are not covered.
- The Chinese Embassy FAQ currently permits multiple visa-free entries and
  states no restriction on entry count, interval or total days. Each entry must
  still fit the policy and pass border inspection.

## Passport scope

- GOV.UK explicitly says its China entry guidance is for a full “British
  citizen” passport.
- The Chinese Embassy policy requires a valid ordinary passport.
- Emergency, temporary and other travel documents do not qualify for
  visa-free entry.
- China states that it does not recognise the British National (Overseas)
  passport as a valid travel or identity document.
- The checked official pages do not clearly enumerate every other British
  nationality category. The public article therefore tells those readers to
  confirm their case with the Chinese Embassy instead of inferring eligibility.

## Deliberate source conflict treatment

The Chinese Embassy FAQ says the ordinary passport must remain valid for at
least the intended stay. GOV.UK advises six months’ validity after arrival and
two blank pages. The guide shows both positions and advises readers to follow
the stricter guidance unless the Chinese Embassy and their carrier confirm
otherwise.

GOV.UK also contains a general sentence about needing a second-entry visa after
a Hong Kong visit. For travellers relying on the newer 30-day visa-free policy,
the article uses the newer Chinese Embassy FAQ: multiple entries are currently
permitted, but returning from Hong Kong or Macao is a fresh mainland entry and
not a guaranteed “reset”.

## 2027 boundary

No official extension beyond 31 December 2026 was found on the checked pages.
The guide therefore does not predict a 2027 extension. It also treats entry on
31 December followed by a stay into January as an edge case that the traveller
should confirm directly.
