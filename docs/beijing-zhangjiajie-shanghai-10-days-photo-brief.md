# Beijing–Zhangjiajie–Shanghai 10-day guide — new-photo brief

## Non-negotiable rule

Do not reuse any image already present on the Homeground website or in another published guide. Every selected file needs a clear owner, commercial-use permission, and an untouched original retained outside the web export.

## Minimum set before public indexing

### 1. Route-opening photograph — required

- Subject: the reality of moving through China, not a generic scenic postcard. Good options include travellers with normal luggage in a clean railway-station setting, a platform/departure-board context with no readable personal data, or an airport-to-hotel arrival moment.
- Purpose: establish immediately that this article is about the whole journey and usable time.
- Orientation: landscape, ideally 3:2 or 16:9.
- Minimum original size: 2400 px on the long edge.
- Composition: useful negative space on one side; no text baked into the image.

### 2. Zhangjiajie variability photograph — required

- Subject: a photograph that helps explain why two days require choices. Useful evidence includes mist or changing visibility, long walking terrain, stairs, shuttles, cable-car access, or visitors shown at a safe distance for scale.
- Avoid: reusing the existing Zhangjiajie guide hero or any of its current derivatives.
- Orientation: landscape 4:3 or 3:2; at least 1800 px on the long edge.

### 3. Homeground planning process — required

- Subject: a genuine route-checking moment—hands over a route calendar, laptop plus printed timings, or a team member comparing dates and bases.
- Faces are optional. Authenticity matters more than production polish.
- Remove or blur names, booking references, phone numbers, email addresses, passport details, supplier rates, and any other client or commercial data before delivery.
- Orientation: landscape 3:2; at least 1800 px on the long edge.

## Useful editorial additions

### 4. Beijing “three full days” photograph — optional

- Subject: a real sightseeing day with human scale—walking at the Great Wall, entering an imperial site, or navigating a large historic complex.
- Avoid: an empty postcard view that says nothing about pace or walking.
- Orientation: landscape 4:3 or 3:2; at least 1800 px on the long edge.

### 5. Shanghai “two days means substitution” photograph — optional

- Subject: a lived central-Shanghai day, arrival movement, neighbourhood walk, or riverside scene with people and spatial context.
- Avoid: a skyline-only image that cannot support the planning argument.
- Orientation: landscape 4:3 or 3:2; at least 1800 px on the long edge.

## Delivery request

For each file, provide:

1. the full-resolution original;
2. photographer or owner name;
3. confirmation that Homeground may use it commercially on the website and social previews;
4. location and approximate date;
5. the names of any identifiable people and their release status.

Do not add a watermark, logo, caption, filter, or AI enlargement before delivery. Web crops, colour correction, responsive formats, alt text and visible credits should be produced from the original after selection.
